⬡ SIGNAL — How It Works
The Core Signal — NMS
NMS
Narrative Momentum Score. A 0–100 number on every story card. Measures how much narrative pressure is building around this story across four independent signals. Green = strong narrative support. Red = weak or fading.
NMS = Trend×0.25 + PM×0.50 + Sentiment×0.10 + Co-occurrence×0.15
Weights shift by regime. In Risk-Off, PM weight rises to 65%.
Trend (ts)
Search velocity, not volume. How much faster people are searching for this topic vs. your 30-session baseline. A z-score — so it measures surprise, not absolute interest.
Source: Google Trends RSS. Fires only when search interest accelerates unusually.
PM Delta (ps)
The most important component (50%). Where prediction market probability is moving, not where it sits. A market moving from 58% to 71% is more informative than one sitting at 71%. That momentum is the signal.
ps = Polymarket EMA delta (0.75) + Kalshi level (0.25) when both match
Kalshi requires 2+ keyword matches and volume guard to qualify.
Sentiment (ss)
Financial language, not social media language. Uses the Loughran-McDonald lexicon — 300+ terms built for SEC filings and earnings calls. Words like "hawkish," "contagion," "covenant breach" score correctly. Generic AFINN would miss them.
Stories with 2+ uncertainty terms get a +4pt boost (unpriced risk = PM opportunity).
Co-occurrence (co)
How many independent sources are covering the same entities. A story covered by Reuters, FT, Bloomberg, WSJ, and Axios is a confirmed narrative — not a single outlet's take. Scored by distinct source count in the cluster.
Requires World Sweep to run for maximum accuracy. Falls back to entity-matching on current feed.
Narrative Velocity
Velocity Badge
Rate of NMS change per session, not price change. A story at NMS 62 rising at +0.12/session is more actionable than a static NMS 78. Velocity appears on cards when a cluster has 2+ sessions of history.
Slope = linear regression over last 5 NMS readings in cluster history
Surge / Rising
Surge = slope >0.08/session. Rising = 0.03–0.08. These often precede prediction market probability moves by 1–2 sessions — narrative builds before markets price it.
Falling / Collapsing
Falling = slope –0.03 to –0.08. Collapsing = below –0.08. A story fading in narrative momentum is losing its ability to move markets regardless of its current NMS level.
NMI — Narrative Momentum Index
NMI
A single daily 0–100 number for aggregate narrative pressure. Answers: is the overall narrative environment building toward something, or releasing? >65 = bullish narrative pressure. <35 = bearish/fear.
NMI = P×0.35 + M×0.30 + D×0.20 + T×0.15
P — Pressure
Average NMS across active clusters, weighted by story count. High P means the stories with the most coverage are scoring high narratively.
M — PM Alignment
Fraction of narrative clusters that have a matching prediction market. High M means sophisticated money is pricing the same narratives you're seeing in news.
D — Diversity
Average distinct sources per cluster. High D means narratives are confirmed across independent outlets — not a single-source story spreading.
T — Trajectory
Net direction: (rising clusters − falling clusters) / total. High T means more clusters are building than fading. Requires 2+ sessions of cluster history to be meaningful.
"T-score prelim" warning shows when fewer than 2 clusters have session history.
Feed-only vs Sweep
NMI is more accurate when World Sweep has run. Feed-only uses clusters built from the current topic only. Sweep-augmented uses clusters from all 9 domains (8,000+ sources via GDELT).
Factor Lab — Validating the Signal
IC
Information Coefficient. Spearman rank correlation between NMS scores and actual returns. IC = 0.05 is the standard quant finance threshold for a meaningful signal. IC = 0.10 is good. IC > 0.15 is strong.
Requires 5+ captures with resolved price returns. Cross-sectional: ranks NMS against rank of subsequent return across all M20 tickers each session.
ICIR
IC Information Ratio. IC divided by its standard deviation. Measures consistency — a signal that's predictive every session scores higher than one that's occasionally brilliant. ICIR > 0.5 = consistent signal.
Excess Return (XR)
Return of a ticker minus QQQ return over the same period. This is the M20-relevant metric — momentum investing is about outperforming the index, not just going up.
Adaptive Weights
Activates at session 30. Uses your own IC data to replace literature priors. If PM Delta IC is 0.15 and Trend IC is 0.02, the system shifts PM weight up and Trend weight down — calibrating to what actually predicted returns in your portfolio.
The weights you see today (PM 50%, Trend 25%) are starting assumptions. Your data replaces them.
Threshold Confidence
◎ Preliminary (<10 sessions): NMS ≥62/≤40 thresholds are literature priors. ◑ Building (10–29): accumulating evidence. ✓ Validated (30+ with IC≥0.05): thresholds confirmed on your portfolio.
A "Validated" rebalancing signal means the NMS thresholds demonstrably predicted M20 outperformance in your data.
Intelligence Brief
Sessions 1–2
CURRENT SNAPSHOT format. No trajectory language — the system doesn't have a baseline yet. Gemini is instructed not to say "rising" or "stable" without data.
Session 3+
Full trajectory brief: THREADS (what's building), PM ALIGNMENT (what markets are pricing), M20 SIGNAL or KEY ACTORS (portfolio context or narrative drivers), WATCH (what to monitor).
M20 SIGNAL vs KEY ACTORS
Finance/Markets/Tech topics → M20 SIGNAL (portfolio-specific). Health/Geopolitics/Science/Politics → KEY ACTORS (who is driving this narrative). Topic-adaptive, not fixed.
Trend Predictions
Auto-generated after each session. Stored in Factor Lab. Uses NMI, velocity, and PM context to generate 3 binary verifiable predictions with 14-day horizons. These build your personal superforecaster track record automatically.

Why 30 sessions?
Statistical stability. Cross-sectional IC needs ~30 observations to produce reliable estimates of whether the signal is genuinely predictive vs. noise. The adaptive weight formula uses IC² — noisy ICs with high variance collapse toward zero. Stable ICs with low variance survive.
